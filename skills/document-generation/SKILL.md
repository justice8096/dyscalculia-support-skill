### ACCOMMODATIONS â€” MATHEMATICS

**Accommodations do NOT reduce conceptual demands but provide access to math curriculum.**

**Calculators and Computational Tools**
- Use of scientific/basic calculator for computation-intensive tasks while still assessing number sense and reasoning
- Speech-to-text for showing work (reduces graphical/writing demands)
- Assistive technology for complex multi-step problems

**Time Accommodations**
- Extended time (1.5x-2x) on math assessments and timed tasks
- Untimed practice during instruction
- Breaks between problems to manage anxiety and fatigue

**Presentation Accommodations**
- Problems read aloud to student (by human or text-to-speech)
- Enlarged print (16pt+) with high contrast (black on white)
- Reduced number of problems per assignment (e.g., 5 representative problems instead of 20)
- Simplified language in word problems; extraneous information removed
- Number highlighted in word problems
- Clear problem formatting (one problem per line, adequate spacing)

**Response Accommodations**
- Verbal explanation of math process instead of written computation
- Pointing/selecting response from options (selected response instead of constructed)
- Typed response instead of handwritten
- Recording of explanations and strategies (audio file)
- Use of assistive technology (speech-to-text, dictation)

**Manipulatives and Visual Supports** (Always Available)
- Access to: base-10 blocks, counters, Rekenrek, number lines, ten-frames, place value charts, measurement tools
- Graph paper for column alignment and organization
- Formula sheets and operation reference guides
- Magnitude comparison charts and visual representations

**Environmental Accommodations**
- Separate, low-distraction testing location
- Preferential seating near front/visual displays
- Reduced background noise and interruptions
- Testing during times of day when student is most alert
- Frequent breaks built into assessments

**Accommodation Monitoring**
- Effectiveness reviewed at [frequency] IEP meetings
- Implementation verified through classroom observation and progress data
- Adjustments made as needed based on student performance

## Dyscalculia Screening Checklist

### DYSCALCULIA SCREENING CHECKLIST

**Student**: [Name] | **Age**: [Age] | **Grade**: [Grade] | **Date**: [Date]
**Completed by**: [Name, Title]

This checklist is used to identify students at risk for dyscalculia and warranting further assessment.

### Number Sense Indicators

**Kindergarten - Grade 1**
- [ ] Difficulty counting to 10 or beyond
- [ ] Unable to produce sets of objects when asked (e.g., "Show me 5 blocks")
- [ ] Cannot identify which is "more" or "less" between two small sets
- [ ] Finger use awkward or unfamiliar (poor finger gnosia)
- [ ] Trouble recognizing numerals on sight (confuses 2 and 5, 6 and 9)
- [ ] Difficulty forming numerals (reversed, poorly sized, inconsistent)

**Grades 2-3**
- [ ] Cannot subitize (instantly recognize) quantities 1-5
- [ ] Persistent counting-by-ones strategy; cannot skip count
- [ ] Slow, effortful single-digit addition/subtraction (still counting fingers)
- [ ] Cannot decompose numbers (8 = 5 + 3) without concrete support
- [ ] Transposing digits or writing numerals backwards
- [ ] Confusion with place value (confuses 12 and 21)

**Grades 4-5**
- [ ] Slow fluency with multi-digit addition/subtraction
- [ ] Cannot accurately perform regrouping/borrowing
- [ ] Weak understanding of multiplication/division concepts
- [ ] Cannot estimate or check reasonableness of answers
- [ ] Reading and interpreting word problems is labored
- [ ] Difficulty learning new computational strategies

**Grades 6+**
- [ ] Persistent weakness in fact fluency (still relies on counting or fingers)
- [ ] Cannot accurately execute multi-step algorithms
- [ ] Difficulty with fractions and decimal concepts
- [ ] Poor spatial visualization of geometric relationships
- [ ] Cannot estimate or perform mental math
- [ ] Algebraic reasoning significantly below grade level

### Computation Indicators

**All Grade Levels**
- [ ] Frequent procedural errors despite understanding concepts
- [ ] Slow computational speed relative to peers
- [ ] Counting-based strategies beyond typical developmental age
- [ ] Difficulty learning and retaining facts through traditional instruction
- [ ] Strong conceptual understanding but weak procedural execution
- [ ] Inconsistent accuracy (correct some days, incorrect others, on same-type problems)

### Associated Features

**Math Anxiety** (All Ages)
- [ ] Avoids math activities when possible
- [ ] Expresses frustration or shutdown when encountering difficult math problems
- [ ] Anxious facial expressions or body language during math lessons
- [ ] Refuses to participate in math instruction or practice
- [ ] Comments like "I'm bad at math" or "I can't do math"

**Comorbidities** (Consider These)
- [ ] Dyslexia or dysgraphia (reading/writing numbers and symbols may be difficult)
- [ ] ADHD (working memory or attention challenges during extended computation)
- [ ] Dyspraxia (difficulty with spatial relationships or coordination affecting computation)

**Response to Intervention**
- [ ] Despite 8+ weeks of Tier 2 small-group intervention, student shows minimal progress
- [ ] Despite 8+ weeks of Tier 3 intensive intervention, deficit persists
- [ ] Peer progress significantly outpaces student's progress on same curriculum

**Teacher Observations**
- [ ] Teacher reports concern about student's math learning
- [ ] Student performs significantly below grade-level standards
- [ ] Performance inconsistent across math domains (strong in geometry, weak in computation, for example)
- [ ] Significant discrepancy between math ability and other academic areas (strong reader, weak in math)

### Screening Outcomes

**Risk Level**:
- [ ] Low Risk: 0-3 indicators; monitor but no immediate action needed
- [ ] Moderate Risk: 4-6 indicators; recommend small-group intervention and progress monitoring
- [ ] High Risk: 7+ indicators; recommend comprehensive evaluation for possible special education

**Recommendation**:
- [ ] Continue general classroom instruction with monitoring
- [ ] Implement targeted Tier 2 intervention for 8 weeks; monitor progress
- [ ] Refer for comprehensive evaluation including cognitive and math achievement assessments
- [ ] Coordinate with school psychologist for evaluation within 2 weeks

**Follow-Up Action**:
- Assessment scheduled for [Date]
- Intervention begins [Date]
- Progress monitoring start date: [Date]
- Re-screening date: [Date]
