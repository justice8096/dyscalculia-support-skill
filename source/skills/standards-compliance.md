# Dyscalculia Standards Compliance

Standards compliance framework for dyscalculia identification, intervention, and accommodation under IDEA, Section 504, and evidence-based best practices (NCTM, CEC).

## IDEA Requirements for Math SLD

The Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) mandates specific requirements for identifying and serving students with math-specific Learning Disabilities.

### Identification Process

#### Comprehensive Evaluation Components
- **Cognitive Assessment** (IQ): WISC-V, WPPSI-IV, or equivalent (minimum 3 subtests)
- **Math-Specific Achievement**: KeyMath-3, TEMA-3, WRAT-5 (math subtest), or WJ-IV Tests of Achievement (math subtests)
- **Processing Deficits**: Assess working memory, processing speed, visual-spatial processing (IPS, Rapid Naming, etc.)
- **Response to Intervention (RTI)**: Document that intensive Tier 2/3 interventions (minimum 8-10 weeks) did not produce adequate progress
- **Classroom Assessment**: Work samples, curriculum-based measurement (CBM) data showing persistent deficit
- **Exclusionary Factors**: Vision/hearing impairment, intellectual disability, emotional disturbance, environmental/economic disadvantage, language proficiency├óŌé¼ŌĆØmust be ruled out

#### IEP Goal Areas (Math-Specific)

**1. Number Sense**
- Quantity recognition and subitizing (3-5 items)
- Magnitude comparison and ordering
- Part-whole relationships and decomposition
- Example Goal: "Student will identify quantities up to 10 without counting and compare two quantities using 'more/less/equal' with 90% accuracy over 2 weeks"

**2. Computation Skills**
- Addition and subtraction (single and multi-digit)
- Multiplication and division concepts and procedures
- Fractions and decimal representation
- Example Goal: "Student will compute single-digit addition problems with sums to 10 using manipulatives with 85% accuracy across 3 trials"

**3. Math Fluency**
- Automaticity with basic facts (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division)
- Speed and accuracy combined
- Example Goal: "Student will fluently retrieve basic addition facts (0-10) at a rate of 40 digits per minute with 90% accuracy by end of year"

**4. Problem Solving**
- Reading and comprehension of math word problems
- Strategy selection and execution
- Solution verification and reasonableness checking
- Example Goal: "Student will solve addition/subtraction word problems with 1-2 steps with 80% accuracy using manipulatives or visual models"

**5. Math Reasoning**
- Explanation of mathematical thinking
- Pattern identification and prediction
- Multi-step reasoning and justification
- Example Goal: "Student will explain addition strategies verbally and in writing with mathematical vocabulary in 3 of 4 opportunities"

### IEP Service Requirements
- **Frequency**: Minimum 3-5 times weekly for foundational gaps (30-60 min sessions)
- **Service Provider**: Certified special education teacher or math specialist trained in remedial instruction
- **Delivery**: Specialized instruction (can be pull-out, push-in, or hybrid)
- **Progress Monitoring**: Data collection at minimum bi-weekly; review at IEP meetings (at least annually)

## Section 504 Accommodations for Dyscalculia

Section 504 provides accommodations to ensure access to math curriculum without reducing conceptual demands.

### Standard Accommodations

**Calculation Tools**
- Calculator use on computation-heavy tasks (while still assessing number sense and reasoning)
- Speech-to-text for showing work (reducing graphical/lexical dyscalculia impact)
- Alternative algorithms or multi-step solutions

**Time Accommodations**
- Extended time on math assessments (1.5x-2x standard time)
- Untimed practice and formative assessment during instruction
- Breaks to manage math anxiety and fatigue

**Presentation Modifications**
- Enlarged print, high-contrast numerals
- Reduced number of problems (e.g., 5 problems instead of 20, assessing same concepts)
- Simplified language in word problems
- Explicit problem structure (highlighting numbers, operations, question)
- Separate assessment from students without disabilities

**Manipulatives and Visual Supports**
- Permanent access to base-10 blocks, counters, Rekenrek, number lines
- Place value charts, decimal/fraction models, measurement tools
- Graph paper for alignment and organization
- Formula sheets and operation reference guides

**Alternative Response Formats**
- Verbal explanation instead of written computation
- Typed response (for dyscalculia + dysgraphia)
- Selected response instead of constructed response when assessing reasoning
- Use of technology (math apps, graphing calculators, CAS)

**Environmental Modifications**
- Separate, low-distraction testing environment
- Seating near front for visual access to board/demonstrations
- Reduced background noise and interruptions
- Frequent breaks to manage anxiety

### 504 Plan Documentation
Must specify: accommodation, when used, by whom, frequency, assessment impact, classroom implementation, and monitoring.

## NCTM Standards

The National Council of Teachers of Mathematics outlines content and process standards essential for all students, with remediation emphasis on foundational standards.

### Five Content Strands

**1. Number and Operations**
- Understand numbers, ways of representing numbers, relationships among numbers
- Understand how numbers are organized and can be used in arithmetic operations
- For dyscalculia: Extended work on subitizing, magnitude, quantity, basic facts, place value
- Standards: Counting, cardinality, comparison, ordering, part-whole, operations as inverse, fact families

**2. Algebra**
- Understand patterns, relations, and functions
- Represent and analyze mathematical situations using algebraic symbols
- For dyscalculia: Sequencing, pattern continuation, simple equations, variable concepts (post-foundational competence)
- Often delayed until number sense is solid

**3. Geometry**
- Analyze characteristics and properties of two- and three-dimensional shapes
- Spatial reasoning, transformations, coordinate geometry
- For dyscalculia: May be a strength area; use to build confidence and spatial visualization
- Often less impaired than number/operations

**4. Measurement**
- Understand measurable attributes of objects and the units, systems, and processes of measurement
- For dyscalculia: Connect to number sense (length as number line), but often challenging due to fractional understanding
- Requires concrete understanding of iteration and comparison

**5. Data Analysis and Probability**
- Formulate questions, collect data, organize and display data, interpret results
- For dyscalculia: Simpler tasks (frequency counts, pictographs, bar graphs) usually accessible; more complex statistics delayed

### Five Process Standards

**Problem Solving**: Formulate problems, apply strategies, validate solutions, generalize solutions
**Reasoning and Proof**: Explanation of mathematical thinking; pattern identification; justification
**Communication**: Expression of ideas verbally and symbolically; interpretation of others' mathematical thinking
**Connections**: Linking new concepts to prior knowledge and across mathematical domains
**Representation**: Use of models, manipulatives, diagrams, symbols to express mathematical ideas

For dyscalculia remediation: Emphasize concrete/representational processes before abstract symbolic work across all strands.

## Assessment Compliance: Tiered Approach

Proper assessment is essential for identification, progress monitoring, and instructional decision-making.

### Tier 1: Screening
**Purpose**: Identify students at risk for math disability
**Instruments**: BRIEF (executive function screening); Key Math-3 Screener; TEMA-3 (brief version); CBM Math Probes
**Timeframe**: Universal screening (fall); follow-up for low performers (winter)
**Decision**: If score < 25th percentile, proceed to Tier 2

### Tier 2: Diagnostic Assessment
**Purpose**: Clarify nature and severity of deficit; inform intervention
**Instruments**: KeyMath-3 (comprehensive); TEMA-3 (full battery); WRAT-5 Math; Woodcock-Johnson IV Tests of Achievement (math subtests)
**Administration**: Individual assessment; 60-90 minutes
**Analysis**: Profile analysis (standard scores, subtest patterns); identify specific deficit areas (number sense, computation, fluency, reasoning)
**Decision**: If significant gap (1.5+ SD below mean), proceed to comprehensive evaluation for special education consideration

### Tier 3: Comprehensive Evaluation (Special Education)
**Purpose**: Establish disability determination and guide IEP development
**Components**: Cognitive assessment (WISC-V, WPPSI-IV); comprehensive achievement assessment; processing assessments; functional behavior assessment; classroom data; RTI documentation
**Timeline**: 60-day timeline per IDEA; may include multiple sessions
**Interpretation**: Review score patterns; rule out exclusionary factors; consider comorbidities; determine IEP eligibility

### Progress Monitoring Assessments
**Frequency**: Minimum bi-weekly during targeted intervention; at least monthly for general classroom monitoring
**Instruments**:
- **Fluency Probes**: Single-digit fact fluency (digits correct per minute)
- **CBM Probes**: Computation or applied problems matching instructional level
- **Concept Assessments**: Mastery of specific concepts (place value, fractions, etc.)
- **Work Sample Analysis**: Qualitative review of student work for error patterns

**Data Use**: If progress rate < 1 correct additional response per week, intensify intervention (increase frequency, change strategy, provide additional support)

## Standards-Based Compliance Audit Checklist

Use this checklist to audit instructional programs, tools, and services for dyscalculia compliance with IDEA, Section 504, and best practices.

### Identification and Evaluation
- [ ] Comprehensive evaluation includes cognitive and math-specific achievement assessments
- [ ] RTI documentation shows 8+ weeks of Tier 2/3 intervention prior to evaluation referral
- [ ] Math-specific subtests analyzed (not just composite scores)
- [ ] Processing deficits (working memory, processing speed, visual-spatial) assessed
- [ ] Exclusionary factors ruled out
- [ ] Timeline meets IDEA requirements (60 days)
- [ ] Parent involvement and informed consent documented

### IEP Development
- [ ] IEP includes specific math goal areas: number sense, computation, fluency, problem solving, reasoning
- [ ] Goals are SMART and measurable
- [ ] Goals aligned with assessment findings and present levels
- [ ] Services specify: type, frequency, duration, delivery model, service provider qualifications
- [ ] Accommodation (not modification) approach clarified
- [ ] Progress monitoring method and frequency specified
- [ ] Collaboration with general education teacher documented
- [ ] Least restrictive environment (LRE) placement justified

### Section 504 Plan
- [ ] Accommodations clearly specified: calculators, extended time, manipulatives, visual supports, reduced problems
- [ ] Accommodation descriptions include when, where, how implemented
- [ ] Assessment accommodations specified
- [ ] Plan reviewed annually and updated as needed
- [ ] Implementation monitored for compliance and effectiveness
- [ ] Student/parent involvement in plan development

### Instructional Program
- [ ] Instruction follows CRA progression: Concrete ├óŌĆĀŌĆÖ Representational ├óŌĆĀŌĆÖ Abstract
- [ ] Multisensory strategies employed (tactile, kinesthetic, visual, auditory, proprioceptive)
- [ ] Number sense prioritized as foundation before computation
- [ ] Manipulatives available and used regularly
- [ ] Instruction addresses identified subtype deficits (verbal, practognostic, lexical, graphical, operational, ideognostical)
- [ ] Math anxiety addressed through environmental and instructional supports
- [ ] Pacing flexible based on mastery, not grade level
- [ ] Evidence-based programs used (TouchMath, Number Worlds, Stern, Singapore Math adaptations, Strategic Math)
- [ ] Data visualizations use bar charts instead of pie charts for comparisons (pie charts require angle/proportion estimation Ś a core dyscalculia deficit)
- [ ] All charts include text summaries, direct data labels, and no 3D effects
- [ ] Charts limited to 3-5 data series; linear representations (number lines, box plots) preferred over radial/circular
- [ ] Numerical values accompanied by plain-language textual alternatives where possible (W3C COGA: "content usable without understanding math concepts")
- [ ] Values rounded to whole numbers unless precision is essential (e.g., currency); unnecessary decimal places removed (GOV.UK dyscalculia guidelines)
- [ ] Percentages accompanied by natural-frequency equivalents (e.g., "25%" shown alongside "about 1 in 4") (W3C COGA Gap Analysis)
- [ ] Long numbers (5+ digits) chunked with spaces or separators for readability

### Progress Monitoring
- [ ] Data collected at minimum bi-weekly
- [ ] Multiple measures used (fluency, concept, application)
- [ ] Data reviewed and decisions made based on progress rate
- [ ] Insufficient progress triggers intervention intensification
- [ ] Progress communicated to student, parents, IEP team
- [ ] Data retained for accountability

### Universal Design for Learning (UDL)
- [ ] Multiple means of representation (manipulatives, diagrams, symbols, verbal explanations)
- [ ] Multiple means of action and expression (verbal, written, manipulative-based responses)
- [ ] Multiple means of engagement (choice, relevance, autonomy, low-stress environment)
- [ ] Accessibility features present (large print, high contrast, clear layouts)

### Teacher Training and Support
- [ ] Teachers trained in dyscalculia characteristics and CRA methodology
- [ ] Professional development documented (training hours, content)
- [ ] Ongoing coaching/mentoring provided
- [ ] Access to resources and tools
- [ ] Collaboration time for planning and data review built in
- [ ] Curriculum materials aligned with evidence-based approaches

### Ethical Standards
- [ ] Non-discriminatory evaluation procedures (culturally responsive, equitable access)
- [ ] Confidentiality maintained
- [ ] Students treated with respect and included in planning when appropriate
- [ ] Parent communication frequent and meaningful
- [ ] Least restrictive environment prioritized

**MEDIUM (50-75% compliance)**

- Should be remediated within 3-6 months
- Example: "Progress monitoring data collection inconsistent"

**LOW (75-90% compliance)**
- Minor gaps; good foundational adherence
