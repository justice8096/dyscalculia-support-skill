### Example Finding (Detailed)

**Finding ID**: F-CRA-001

**Standard/Law**: CRA Methodology (Evidence-Based Best Practice); IDEA (provision of evidence-based interventions)

**Severity**: [ X ] CRITICAL [ ] HIGH [ ] MEDIUM [ ] LOW

**Category**: Instructional Method

**Element**: Concrete stage implementation in CRA progression

**Description**: Review of the "MathPro Elementary" curriculum shows that instruction begins at the representational stage with pictorial models and moves quickly to abstract symbolic work. The curriculum includes no concrete manipulative stage. Teacher guidance explicitly states, "Begin with the ten-frame illustration; students can visualize without physical materials." Lessons 1-5 of Unit 1 (Number Sense, K) show no manipulative use. By Unit 2, students are expected to write numerals symbolically without prior concrete experience.

**Impact**: Dyscalculic students require extended, repeated concrete stage experience to develop neural pathways for quantity understanding. Skipping the concrete stage is the primary cause of intervention failure in dyscalculia remediation. Students who enter the program with weak number sense cannot learn by visualization alone; they need hands-on manipulation. This curriculum violates the core principle of evidence-based dyscalculia instruction and will result in student failure, frustration, math anxiety, and lack of progress.

**Evidence**:
- Curriculum review: Units 1-3 reviewed; no manipulative mention until Unit 4
- Teacher manual statement: "Concrete materials are optional; visual models are primary instruction"
- Classroom observation: No base-10 blocks, counters, or manipulatives visible in three observed classrooms using this curriculum
- Student interviews: Three Grade 1 students reported, "We don't use blocks; we just draw the pictures"

**Remediation**:
1. Redesign Units 1-3 to include foundational concrete stage using standard manipulatives (base-10 blocks, counters, Rekenrek, number line)
2. Provide at least 3-4 lessons of concrete manipulation per concept before moving to representational stage
3. Revise teacher manual to emphasize manipulative use and provide lesson examples with manipulatives
4. Procure manipulative kits for all classrooms (estimated cost: $X per classroom)
5. Provide professional development (2-3 hours) on CRA implementation
6. Establish progress monitoring to verify students demonstrate concrete stage mastery before advancing

**Effort Estimate**: [ ] Small (1-4 hours) [X] Large (21+ hours) [ ] Ongoing

**Timeline for Remediation**: 6-8 weeks for curriculum redesign; 2 weeks for teacher PD; materials procurement 2 weeks
